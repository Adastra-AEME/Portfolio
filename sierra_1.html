System Integrated Environmental Remote Research Aircraft(SIERRA-B)

Background

The SIERRA-B is a 480 lb. unmanned aeriel system (UAS) that serves as a platform that can carry out missions for different types scientific payloads. Having a modular design and capability to exchange between a small or large nose cones allows the system to optimize its payload volume for a variety of payloads.

Aircraft Capabilities:
- Service Ceiling: 15,000 [ft.]
- Payload Capacity: 100 [lbs.]
- Max Cruise Speed: 70 [kts]
- Max Range: 527 [NM]
- Endurance: 8.8 [hrs]
- Power: 36 [HP]

Lead Mechanical Engineer

As the Lead Mechanical Engineer of the SIERRA-B Project my main role was focused on the design, development, and integration of mechanical components and aircraft payloads. I served as the Configuration Management Officer (CMO), responsible for maintaining the data repository to support component/payload installation and configuration control. In my role I contributed to the team within the following subject areas:

- CAD Modeling
- Finite Element Analysis (FEA).
- Structural Testing
- Payload Integration
- Flight Operations
- Mission Deployments

My Contributions:

CAD Modeling

The SIERRA-B is coninuously evolving as science payloads are integrated into the aircraft for different missions. Designing of new components are done through SolidWorks CAD modeling.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Ensure the SIERRA-B Center-of-Gravity (CG) is within the bounds defined by the control pilot for a specific aircraft configuration.

Structural Testing

Payload Integration
